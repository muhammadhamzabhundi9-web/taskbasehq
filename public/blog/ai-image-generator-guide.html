2
Write an Effective Prompt

The quality of your AI image depends heavily on how well you describe what you want. A good prompt has several components:

❌ WEAK PROMPT (vague):
a business photo
✅ STRONG PROMPT (detailed):
Professional business woman in her 30s, sitting at a modern glass desk in a minimalist office, wearing a navy blazer, confident smile, shallow depth of field, soft natural window lighting, 4K ultra-realistic photography, Canon EOS R5

Notice how the strong prompt specifies: subject description, setting, clothing, expression, photography style, lighting, technical specs. Each detail helps the AI generate exactly what you need.

3
Master Prompt Components

Subject

Be specific about what you want. Instead of "a dog," try "a golden retriever puppy, 8 weeks old, sitting in autumn leaves."

Style

Specify the visual style: photorealistic, watercolor, oil painting, digital art, minimalist, vintage, corporate, cinematic.

watercolor illustration, soft pastel colors, hand-painted texture, botanical art style

Lighting

Lighting dramatically changes the mood of an image: golden hour, studio lighting, neon lights, candlelight, moonlight, soft diffused light.

dramatic side lighting, long shadows, high contrast, film noir atmosphere

Camera & Technical Terms

Adding photography terms helps achieve specific looks:

85mm lens, f/1.8 aperture, bokeh background, shot on Sony A7IV, RAW format quality

Negative Prompts

Some tools allow you to specify what you DON'T want: blurry, distorted, low quality, extra limbs, watermark.

Pro Tips for Better AI Images

💡 Tip 1: Use Specific Artists or Styles as References
Referencing well-known styles helps: "in the style of minimalist Scandinavian design," "inspired by Bauhaus architecture," "editorial photography like Vogue magazine." This gives the AI a clear aesthetic reference.
💡 Tip 2: Iterate and Refine
Your first generation rarely needs to be your last. If the result is 70% there, refine your prompt. Add more detail to what's wrong, or use the AI Photo Editor tool to modify specific elements of an image you like.
💡 Tip 3: Aspect Ratio Matters
Specify the aspect ratio in your prompt: "16:9 widescreen landscape," "9:16 vertical for Instagram stories," "1:1 square format." This prevents awkward cropping when you use the image.
💡 Tip 4: Use Background Remover After Generation
Generate your product or subject, then use the Background Remover tool to get a clean cutout. This is far faster than manual Photoshop editing and works accurately even on complex edges like hair.
💡 Tip 5: Combine Multiple Tools
Generate an image → Enhance with Photo Enhancer → Remove background → Add to your design. Using multiple AI tools in sequence produces professional results that rivals expensive design agencies.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Prompts That Are Too Vague

The AI can't read your mind. "Make it look professional" means nothing — specify exactly what makes something professional in your context: clean lines, corporate colors, business attire, formal setting.

2. Ignoring Quality Tiers

Using Standard quality for print materials will produce pixelated results. Match your quality tier to your use case — save credits on social media posts, use 4K Ultra for anything that will be printed large.

3. Not Iterating

AI image generation is rarely a one-shot process. Professional results come from iterating — generating multiple versions, combining the best elements, and refining your prompts based on results.

4. Overlooking Commercial License Requirements
